Acoustic Preservation

Every environment possesses a sonic fingerprint, a shifting composition of biological, geological, and human sound. Yet while landscapes are photographed and mapped with precision, their acoustic identity is rarely documented.

Aurafact approaches environmental recordings as archival specimens. Each capture preserves a moment in the sonic evolution of a place, transforming sound into a permanent, searchable record for research, reflection, and cultural memory.
